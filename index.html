Weekend of AR & VR

A fun-filled two-day event consisting of workshops, mini-competitions, and Experience Centers showcasing the best AR/VR experiences on a variety of platforms and headsets. October 20-21, 2018 @ UMD CSIC Bldg.

HIGHLIGHTS

The Cool Stuff You'll Do

Learn

Take part in workshops designed to teach you the best AR & VR development practices. No prior coding experience necessary!

Experience

Stop by our exclusive Experience Centers for a guided one-on-one tour of some of the most breathtaking AR & VR experiences currently available.

Brainstorm

Rapidly generate and build off of innovative AR & VR concepts you never previously considered. Maybe it will be the next billion dollar idea! Who knows?

Create

Work around the clock to prototype your idea with your team, using your newfound ninja coding skills. But if you get stuck, don't worry; we still have mentors to help you out.

Compete

Pitch and demo your masterpieces to the public. You may even win a prize... (hint, hint, hint)

ROLES

Three Ways You Can Be Involved

  • 1
    As an Attendee

    Expand your skillset and learn how to create AR & VR apps. Take part in mini-competitions and win unique prizes. Play around with cool technologies and make your dreams come to life. As you can see, it's quite fun to be an attendee. Register now so you can #DefineTheFuture.

    As a Volunteer

    Volunteers are the star of the show: they help run day-of logistics. From running sign-ins to serving food to giving guided tours of apps and showing everyone how to use the AR/VR equipment (training will be provided), they're the ones to thank for keeping the event running smoothly. Interested? Register now below!

  • 3
    As an Organizer

    Want to be even more involved beyond the course of this single weekend? Join our organizing team as we put together this event and other AR/VR events, like our hackathon in the spring. Multiple technical and nontechnical positions available. Contact hello@realityhacks.io for more details.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can attend the event?

Any current undergraduate or graduate student at the University of Maryland, College Park is eligible to attend our event. Unfortunately, we are unable to open registrations to high school students or other college students at this time.

What should I bring?

In order to check-in, you must bring your UMD University ID Card on both days. We also recommend you bring a laptop, chargers, and whatever else you normally bring with you to class.

How much does this event cost?

Zero. Zip. Nada. Just get yourself over to the CSIC building and we'll take care of the rest!

Do I have to register before the event?

Only those who have completed registration will be allowed to check in to the event, so we highly, highly recommend you do so. Walk-ins are still welcomed, however they will be placed on the waitlist until half-way through the opening ceremony and will only be let inside on a space-available basis.

What if I don't have a team or an idea?

No worries! Most people don't. We will have an idea brainstorm and team formation session towards the end of our first day. In fact, many attendees will find inspiration for their idea and meet people with like interests while taking part in the first day's activities.

What if I don't know how to code?

No prior coding experience is necessary to attend our event. We'll have plenty of workshops for both beginners and experts so that everyone is able to learn and create a meaningful project.

Will you have AR/VR hardware for me to use?

Yes. Please take a look at all the neat hardware we offer in our tools section of this webpage.

What if my question is not listed on here?

Then it is not frequently asked! jk ;)
But seriously, we realize there may not have answered all your questions, so feel free to reach out to us at hello@realityhacks.io or on Facebook or Twitter.
